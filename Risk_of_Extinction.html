Animals in the Ocean Facing the Risk of Extinction

Exploring the World’s Most Threatened Ecosystems

Introduction

The ocean is home to a vast range of species, many of which are now endangered due to human activities. Major threats include plastic pollution, oil spills, toxic waste, and climate change.

Overfishing and destructive practices further reduce marine biodiversity, causing long-term ecological imbalance.

Blue Whale

The blue whale is endangered due to ship strikes and climate change.

Great Hammerhead Shark

Population decline due to fishing and shark fin trade.

Leatherback Sea Turtle

Threatened by plastic pollution, egg harvesting, and climate change.

Hawksbill Sea Turtle

Critically endangered due to habitat destruction and poaching.

Dugong

Endangered due to habitat loss, boat collisions, and fishing nets.

Vaquita

Nearly extinct due to illegal fishing and net entanglement.

Napoleon Wrasse

Endangered due to overfishing and habitat degradation.

Giant Manta Ray

Threatened by fishing and environmental changes.
