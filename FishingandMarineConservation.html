Fishing and Marine Conservation.

The ocean is larger than 70 percent of the Earth and is very crucial in supporting life on earth. It hosts millions of species of the sea, controls the global climate, generates a huge part of the oxygen we breathe, and sustains billions of people through food and livelihoods. The ocean is being pressured more and more by human activities that include overfishing, pollution, habitat destruction, and climate change, despite its importance. To maintain healthy marine ecosystems, sustainable fishing practices and strong conservation efforts are more important than ever.



Sustainable Fishing Practices

Sustainable fishing is aimed at the harvesting of fish and other seafood in manners that do not affect long-term health of marine communities and ecosystems. This is aimed at making sure that fish stocks are not depleted by making sure that they are abundant and capable of reproduction.

Nowadays over a third of the world fish stocks is overfished and this breaks food chains in the vicinity and endangers biodiversity. To deal with this, there are various strategies that governments and organizations have put forth:

  • Catch limits are established to regulate the amount of fish that can be caught.
  • Seasonal closures will give fish a chance to breed and recuperate.
  • Eco-friendly and selective fishing equipment minimizes bycatch, or the unintentional trapping of non-target species including turtles, dolphins and seabirds.
  • Supervision and implementation make sure that fishing laws are adhered to.

Ocean Conservation Efforts

Ocean conservation is a process where marine environments and damaged ecosystems are conserved. Sound ecology requires healthy habitats to support marine life and balance in the ecology.

The major marine ecosystems are the coral reefs, mangrove forests, seagrass beds, and deep-sea. These habitats not only offer shelter, breeding areas and food to numerous species but also have other advantages like coastal protection and carbon storage.

The establishment of Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) is one of the best conservation tools as it allows limiting or closely controlling human activity. Presently, the world only has approximately 8 percent of its oceans under protection, yet the researchers suggest a need to have at least 30 percent of the oceans under protection by 2030 in order to achieve significant conservation.

One of the key challenges is pollution. An average of more than 8 million tons of plastic waste finds its way into the ocean every year and affects marine animals by ingestion and entanglement. Moreover, climate change is leading to the warming and acidification of the oceans that harm ecosystems such as the coral reefs. Conservation activities are aimed at fighting these threats by:

  • Minimizing consumption of plastic and enhancing waste management.
  • Implementation of environmental protection laws.
  • Restoring damaged habitats
  • Protecting endangered species
  • Encouraging coastal development and sustainable tourism.
